# Whole-book timeline

Timeline events are derived primarily from accepted Fact and graph evidence frozen by a
BookSnapshot. Each event keeps chapter/version identity, a deterministic key, sequence,
optional story time and location, participants, causal links, confidence, and bounded
evidence. Candidate, rejected, superseded, and future versions are excluded.

Rules detect order reversal, simultaneous locations, implausible travel markers, action
after death, use after destruction, cause after consequence, numeric date regression,
duplicate first events, and abnormal chapter time spans. Findings are deterministic and
explainable. Low-confidence evidence cannot create a critical finding. This is bounded
structured inference, not a claim of complete natural-language temporal reasoning.
